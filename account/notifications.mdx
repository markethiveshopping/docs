MarketHive's notification system keeps you informed at every stage of your job search — from the moment you unlock a contact to when a new listing appears that matches your interests. Notifications are delivered by email to the address registered on your account, and you stay in full control of which ones you receive. Taking a few minutes to configure your preferences means you'll never miss a lead that matters to you.

## Types of Notifications

MarketHive sends five categories of notifications, each designed to support a specific part of your experience on the platform.

### Unlock Confirmation

The unlock confirmation email is sent the instant your payment is processed. It contains the full contact details for the listing you just unlocked — the same information stored in your [Payment History](/account/payment-history). This email serves as both your receipt and your primary delivery mechanism for the contact details you paid for.

<Warning>
If you disable unlock confirmation emails, contact details will only be displayed on-screen at the moment of payment. You will **not** receive an email copy, and if you close the page before saving the details, you'll need to retrieve them from your Payment History page. To avoid any risk of losing access to contact information, it is strongly recommended to keep unlock confirmation emails enabled at all times.
</Warning>

### New Job Alerts

New job alerts notify you by email when listings that match your interests are posted on MarketHive. You can configure these alerts based on:

- **Saved searches** — keyword and location combinations you've bookmarked
- **Job categories** — industries or roles you're interested in

<Tip>
Set up job alerts for your target role and location so new opportunities surface in your inbox automatically — without needing to check the platform manually every day. The sooner you spot a relevant listing, the sooner you can unlock and reach out to the employer.
</Tip>

### Saved Job Updates

When you save a listing to review later, MarketHive monitors it for changes. You'll receive a notification if the employer:

- **Edits the listing** — updates the job description, requirements, or contact details
- **Closes the listing** — the role is no longer accepting applicants
- **Features the listing** — the employer promotes it to a highlighted position on the platform

This helps you act quickly before a listing closes and avoids wasting time pursuing a role that's already filled.

### Platform Announcements

Platform announcements are low-frequency emails covering new features, policy changes, or major updates to how MarketHive works. You can opt out of these at any time without affecting your other notification types.

### Employer Notifications

If you have an employer account, you'll receive a notification every time a job seeker pays to unlock your listing's contact details. This gives you a heads-up that a candidate may be reaching out soon, so you can be ready to respond. You can choose to receive these alerts in real time or as a daily digest.

## Managing Your Notification Preferences

To update which notifications you receive:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Notification Settings">
    Click your avatar in the top-right corner and select **Notification Settings** from the account menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review each notification type">
    You'll see a list of all available notification categories, each with a toggle switch showing its current state (enabled or disabled).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle notifications on or off">
    Click the toggle next to any notification type to enable or disable it. Changes take effect immediately — no save button required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set delivery preferences (where available)">
    For notification types that support it — such as employer unlock alerts — choose between **instant** delivery or a **daily digest** using the dropdown next to the toggle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Account and security alerts — such as password change confirmations and login notifications — cannot be disabled. These are sent automatically to protect your account regardless of your notification preferences.
</Note>
