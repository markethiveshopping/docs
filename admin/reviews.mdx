Reviews are a core trust signal on MarketHive. When job seekers pay to unlock a listing, they gain the ability to leave a rating and written review of their experience — helping future job seekers make informed decisions about which listings are worth unlocking. As an admin, your job is to keep the review system honest by approving genuine feedback, rejecting policy-violating content, and investigating disputes from employers. A well-moderated review system protects job seekers, rewards quality employers, and reinforces the value of the platform.

## How Reviews Work

After a job seeker unlocks a listing's contact details, they are eligible to submit a star rating (1–5) and a written review of their experience. Reviews are linked to the specific listing they relate to and appear publicly on that listing's page once approved.

Reviews are **anonymous to other job seekers** — no name or identifying information is shown alongside a published review. However, the reviewer's account is fully visible to admins in the moderation interface, enabling you to verify the review's authenticity and take action on bad-faith submissions.

<Note>
  Although reviews are displayed anonymously to other users, you can always see the reviewer's account details in the admin panel. This visibility exists solely for moderation purposes — do not disclose reviewer identities to employers or other users.
</Note>

## Review Moderation Queue

Every review submitted by a job seeker enters the moderation queue before it is published. The queue is accessible at **Admin → Reviews** and displays each review's star rating, a preview of the written content, the listing it relates to, and the submission date.

New reviews should be moderated promptly. Delays in the review queue mean job seekers cannot see fresh feedback, which reduces the trust value that reviews provide. Aim to clear the queue daily.

## Moderating a Review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin → Reviews">
    Open the admin dashboard and select **Reviews** from the left-hand navigation menu. The moderation queue at the top of the page lists all reviews with a **Pending** status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a pending review">
    Click on a review entry to open the full moderation view. This shows the complete written review, the star rating, the listing it was submitted for, and the reviewer's account details (visible to you only).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for policy violations">
    Assess the review against the following criteria:
    - **Spam** — the review is repetitive, irrelevant, or clearly automated.
    - **Profanity or offensive language** — the review contains language that violates the platform's content standards.
    - **Off-topic content** — the review doesn't relate to the job listing or the experience of contacting the employer.
    - **Bad faith** — the reviewer has no record of unlocking the listing, or shows signs of coordinated review manipulation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or Reject">
    - Click **Approve** to publish the review. It becomes visible on the listing immediately.
    - Click **Reject** to decline the review. Enter a rejection reason before confirming — this is recorded in the moderation log. The reviewer is notified that their review was not approved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing a Published Review

If a published review is reported by an employer or another user after it went live, you can investigate and remove it if it's found to violate platform policy.

**To remove a published review:**

1. Navigate to **Admin → Reviews** and select the **Published** tab.
2. Use the search bar to locate the review by listing name, reviewer, or keyword.
3. Open the review and click **Remove Review** from the action menu.
4. Enter a reason for removal — this is logged in the moderation record.
5. Confirm the removal. The review is immediately hidden from the listing.

<Warning>
  Removing a published review is a significant moderation action. Only remove a review if it clearly violates platform policy. Removing legitimate negative reviews at an employer's request is not permitted and undermines the integrity of the review system.
</Warning>

## Handling Review Disputes from Employers

Employers can formally dispute a review they believe is inaccurate or policy-violating by submitting a dispute through their account. Disputes appear in the **Review Disputes** tab under **Admin → Reviews**.

To handle a dispute:

1. Open the dispute and read the employer's reasoning.
2. Re-examine the original review alongside the reviewer's account and unlock history.
3. Make an independent judgment — do not take the employer's claim at face value.
4. If the review violates policy, remove it and notify the employer that action was taken.
5. If the review is legitimate, dismiss the dispute and notify the employer that the review will remain published.
6. Mark the dispute as **Resolved** with a note explaining your decision.

<Info>
  Employers are allowed a limited number of disputes per billing period to discourage abuse of the disputes process. If an employer repeatedly disputes legitimate negative reviews, flag their account for closer monitoring.
</Info>

## Platform Review Policy Summary

All reviews on MarketHive must meet the following standards:

- **Based on genuine experience** — the reviewer must have unlocked the listing's contact details before submitting a review.
- **Relevant to the listing** — the review should reflect the job seeker's experience with the listing's accuracy and the quality of the employer's contact details.
- **Free from prohibited content** — no spam, profanity, personal attacks, discriminatory language, or promotional material.
- **One review per listing per user** — each job seeker can leave one review per listing they have unlocked.

Reviews that do not meet these standards should be rejected during moderation or removed if discovered after publication.

<Tip>
  Pay attention to patterns in negative reviews across listings. If multiple job seekers report that a specific listing's contact details are outdated or unreachable, treat this as a signal to audit that listing. Reach out to the employer to request updated contact information — or pause the listing until valid details are confirmed. Protecting the integrity of the contact details that job seekers pay to unlock is fundamental to the platform's value.
</Tip>
