As an admin, you have full control over every job listing on MarketHive. Your role is to ensure that all live listings are accurate, policy-compliant, and genuinely useful to job seekers who pay to unlock them. From the **Admin → Job Listings** panel, you can review new submissions, edit existing listings, feature standout opportunities, and remove anything that violates platform standards.

## Listing Moderation Queue

Every new listing submitted by an employer enters the moderation queue with a **Pending Review** status before it goes live. The queue appears at the top of the Job Listings panel and shows the listing title, employer name, submission date, and a quick-view link.

Work through the queue in chronological order so that employers aren't waiting unnecessarily long for their listings to go live. Aim to clear pending listings within one business day.

## Approving or Rejecting a Listing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Admin → Job Listings">
    Open the admin dashboard and select **Job Listings** from the left-hand navigation menu. The moderation queue at the top of the page shows all listings with a **Pending Review** status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a pending listing">
    Click on a listing title to open the full listing detail view. Review the job title, description, contact details, compensation, and any attached media.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for policy compliance">
    Assess the listing against the following criteria before making a decision:
    - **No misleading information** — the job title and description must accurately represent the role.
    - **Valid contact details** — the employer's contact information must be present, reachable, and not obviously fraudulent.
    - **Appropriate content** — the listing must be free of offensive language, discriminatory requirements, or prohibited job categories.
    - **Not a duplicate** — check that the employer hasn't already posted an identical or near-identical listing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or Reject">
    - Click **Approve** to publish the listing immediately. It becomes **Active** and visible to job seekers.
    - Click **Reject** to decline the listing. You must enter a rejection reason before confirming — this reason is sent directly to the employer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  When you reject a listing, the employer receives an automated notification containing the rejection reason you provide. Write the reason clearly and constructively — it is the employer's primary signal for understanding what needs to change before resubmitting.
</Warning>

## Editing a Listing on Behalf of an Employer

If a listing has a minor policy issue that doesn't warrant a full rejection — such as a formatting problem, a typo in the contact email, or a missing detail — you can edit it directly rather than sending it back to the employer.

To edit a listing:

1. Open the listing detail view from the Job Listings panel.
2. Click **Edit Listing** in the top-right action menu.
3. Make the necessary corrections in the listing editor.
4. Click **Save & Approve** to publish the corrected listing, or **Save as Draft** to review it again before approving.

<Note>
  Any edits you make are logged in the listing's activity history with your admin username and a timestamp. This ensures a clear audit trail for all admin-initiated changes.
</Note>

## Featuring a Listing

Featured listings are promoted to the top of search results and category pages, giving them greater visibility. Use featuring to highlight high-quality listings that represent strong opportunities for job seekers.

To feature a listing:

1. Open the listing detail view.
2. Click the **Feature Listing** toggle in the listing settings panel.
3. Optionally set an expiry date for the featured status — after this date, the listing returns to standard placement automatically.
4. Click **Save**.

<Tip>
  Reserve featured status for listings with complete, accurate information and verified employers. Featuring low-quality listings undermines job seekers' trust in promoted content.
</Tip>

## Removing or Closing a Listing

You can remove or close a listing at any time from the listing detail view.

- **Close a listing** — use this when a role has been filled or the employer requests removal. The listing moves to **Closed** status and is hidden from job seekers, but the data is retained.
- **Remove a listing** — use this for listings that violate platform policy and should not be reinstated. Removed listings are archived and no longer accessible to the employer or job seekers.

To take either action, open the listing, click the **Actions** dropdown in the top-right corner, and select **Close Listing** or **Remove Listing**. Confirm your choice in the dialog.

## Bulk Actions

When you need to process multiple listings at once, use the bulk action toolbar in the Job Listings panel.

1. Select the checkboxes next to the listings you want to action.
2. Click the **Bulk Actions** dropdown above the list.
3. Choose **Approve**, **Reject**, or **Remove**.
4. If rejecting in bulk, enter a shared rejection reason that applies to all selected listings, or apply individual reasons one at a time.
5. Confirm the action.

<Note>
  Bulk actions are best suited for clear-cut cases such as spam waves or identical duplicate submissions. For nuanced moderation decisions, review each listing individually to ensure the right action is taken.
</Note>

## Listing Status Reference

<Accordion title="Pending Review">
  The listing has been submitted by an employer and is waiting for admin approval. It is not visible to job seekers yet.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Active">
  The listing has been approved and is live on the platform. Job seekers can view the listing and pay to unlock the employer's contact details.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Paused">
  The listing has been temporarily hidden from job seekers, either by the employer or by an admin. It can be reactivated at any time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Closed">
  The listing has been closed, typically because the role has been filled or the employer requested removal. Closed listings are not visible to job seekers and cannot be unlocked.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Rejected">
  The listing was reviewed and declined by an admin. The employer has been notified with a rejection reason and may edit and resubmit the listing.
</Accordion>
