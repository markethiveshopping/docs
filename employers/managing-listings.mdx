Your employer dashboard is the control centre for every job listing you post on MarketHive. From a single screen you can monitor performance, make changes to live listings, pause roles that are temporarily on hold, and close positions once they're filled. Everything you need to stay on top of your hiring activity is in one place.

## Dashboard Overview

When you sign in and open the dashboard, you'll see a summary of all your listings and their key metrics:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Active Listings" icon="circle-check" href="#understanding-listing-status">
    The number of jobs currently live and visible to job seekers on MarketHive.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Total Views" icon="eye" href="#dashboard-overview">
    How many times job seekers have viewed your listing detail pages across all active and past listings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Unlocks Count" icon="lock-open" href="#dashboard-overview">
    The number of job seekers who have paid to unlock your contact details for each listing — your most meaningful engagement signal.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Listing Status" icon="flag" href="#understanding-listing-status">
    The current state of each listing: Active, Paused, Closed, or Under Review.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Edit a Listing

Keep your listings accurate as roles evolve. You can update any field — including salary, description, job type, and contact information — at any time on a live listing.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your employer dashboard">
    Sign in and navigate to **My Listings** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the listing you want to edit">
    Find the listing in your list and click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) on the right side of the row.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    Update any fields you need — job title, description, salary, location, job type, or contact details. All fields are editable after publishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and republish">
    Click **Save Changes**. Your updated listing goes live immediately with no re-approval delay for standard edits.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  If you update your **contact details** on a live listing, new job seekers who unlock your listing from that point forward will receive the updated information. However, contact details already delivered to job seekers who unlocked your listing before the change are **not retroactively updated** — those seekers received the information that was live at the time of their unlock.
</Info>

## How to Pause a Listing

Pausing a listing temporarily removes it from public search results and browse pages without permanently closing the role. Use this when you need to slow down applications — for example, when you have enough candidates to review and don't want to collect more right now.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to My Listings">
    From your dashboard, select **My Listings** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the listing to pause">
    Click the **Actions** menu (three-dot icon) next to the listing you want to pause.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Pause Listing'">
    Select **Pause Listing** from the dropdown. The listing status changes to **Paused** and it disappears from public view immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resume when ready">
    To make the listing live again, open the same Actions menu and select **Resume Listing**. It returns to active status instantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Delete or Close a Listing

Once a role is filled or no longer available, close the listing to remove it permanently from the platform.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to My Listings">
    From your dashboard, select **My Listings** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Actions menu">
    Click the **Actions** menu (three-dot icon) next to the listing you want to close.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select 'Close Listing'">
    Choose **Close Listing**. You'll be asked to confirm — click **Yes, Close** to proceed. The listing status changes to **Closed** and it is removed from public view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding Listing Status

Every listing on MarketHive has one of four statuses:

<Accordion title="Active">
  Your listing is live and fully visible to job seekers. It appears in search results, category pages, and — if featured — at the top of relevant pages. Job seekers can view the public details and pay to unlock your contact information.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Paused">
  Your listing is temporarily hidden from job seekers but is not deleted. All listing data, analytics, and unlock history are preserved. You can resume a paused listing at any time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Closed">
  The listing has been permanently closed, either by you or because the role was marked as filled. Closed listings no longer appear in public searches but remain visible in your dashboard history for reference.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Under Review">
  Your listing has been flagged for manual review by the MarketHive moderation team. This typically happens with new accounts or listings containing certain keywords. Your listing will not be publicly visible until the review is complete. You'll receive an email notification once approved or if any changes are required.
</Accordion>

## How to Repost a Closed Listing

If a role opens up again after you've closed the listing, you can repost it without filling in all the details from scratch.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the closed listing">
    In **My Listings**, filter by **Closed** status to locate the listing you want to repost.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Repost'">
    Select the **Repost** button next to the listing. A copy of the original listing opens in the editing form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and update the details">
    Check that all information is still accurate — especially salary, location, and contact details. Make any necessary changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the reposted listing">
    Click **Publish Listing**. The reposted job goes live as a new active listing with a fresh view count and unlock count.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Keep your listings up to date.** An inaccurate listing — wrong salary, outdated contact details, or a role that's already been filled — frustrates job seekers who pay to unlock your information and find it no longer relevant. Regularly review your active listings and pause or close any that are no longer accurate. This protects your reputation and ensures the candidates who reach out are genuinely matched to the role.
</Tip>
